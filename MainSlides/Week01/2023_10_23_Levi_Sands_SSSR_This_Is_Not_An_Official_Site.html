## Terminology Disclaimer Despite our data mostly consisting of members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints we will use the terms Mormon/Mormonism as they are common in the academic literature and as a way to acknowledge the broader Latter-day Saint movement
## Mormon Theology - Latter-day Saints don't have a systematic theology (Blythe 2020) - Mythology and narrative theology inherited from Joseph Smith (Flake 2007) - Personal revelation and doctrine as taught by church leaders can come in conflict (Knoll and Riess 2020) - These characteristics of Mormon theology leaves many areas of differing beliefs, speculations and areas for discussion

## Methods - Topic Modeling - Topic Modeling As a Method of Analysis - Several steps to get topic model outputs, each of those steps have tradeoffs: embedding, dimensionality reduction, clustering, tokenization, weighting scheme, and sometimes more (Grootendorst 2022) - Newer pre-trained embedding models use context to keep more semantic information (Devlin et al. 2019) - "'[T]opics' (clusters of words associated with a single theme) emerge inductively based on patterns of co-occurrence of words in a corpus."
